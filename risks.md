# Portative Organ Risks

- Bellows pulse may be too strong for the reservoir to smooth, producing an
  uneven or chirping pipe attack. See `design.md` Wind System Reasoning.
- Reservoir response may be too sluggish, making pipe attack feel disconnected
  from the player's pump stroke.
- Pallets may leak, stick, slap, or require excessive touch weight; facing
  material and return method (spring vs. weight) are undecided. See
  `decision-record.md` Open Questions.
- Windchest channels may couple notes so that multiple-key behavior becomes
  unstable; this is untested until a wind-system mule exists.
- Pipe feet or mouths could be damaged by the portable case layout once a real
  pipe rank exists.
- Player support mode (strap, lap, or table) may twist the windchest or change
  pallet sealing; no support mode has been chosen yet.
- A future second rank would double wind demand before a single-rank mule is
  proven; `bom.csv` reserves this as a later option only (`PO-BOM-011`).
- No pipe length, wind pressure, or tuning value exists anywhere in this
  packet; publishing any such number without a measured mule or reviewed
  design table would misrepresent readiness. See `design.md` V5 Boundary and
  `visual-output-register.csv`.
- Live sourcing has not been checked (`sourcing.csv` is all TBD), so costs and
  lead times are placeholders only.
- Remain at L2 until the non-playing wind-system mule (`PO-BOM-016`), pallet
  sealing, and pipe-scale research are measured and reviewed.
